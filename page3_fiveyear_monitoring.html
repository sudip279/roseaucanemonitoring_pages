Fall Survey — Post-Season Senescence Mapping

Orthomosaic Seasonal Contrast Water Level Impact

Fall survey documents post-peak senescence and die-back dynamics. Low water levels in fall caused the east side of the drone field to become completely dry, reducing accessibility for boat-based ground sampling. The drone sampling method developed by the team was deployed to overcome this challenge. Direct comparison with Summer 2023 reveals intra-annual vegetation dynamics.

Observation
Seasonal water level variation substantially alters site accessibility and reflectance signatures — requires multi-date analysis to separate phenological from structural change

October 2024 — Full Deep Learning Classification

U-Net Model 8 Classes Accuracy Assessed

The October 2024 flight produced the project's most complete classification result to date. Agisoft Metashape-processed orthomosaic was classified using a U-Net deep learning model, successfully separating 8 distinct land cover classes with 92% overall accuracy and a Kappa coefficient of 0.89. A comprehensive confusion matrix was generated confirming per-class reliability.

Environmental Context
Disturbance Events Captured in the Monitoring Window
The five-year monitoring window deliberately spans multiple disturbance cycles to quantify Roseau cane response and recovery trajectories.
🌨
Freeze Events
Winter freeze episodes cause rapid die-back in Roseau cane stands, creating distinct spectral signatures visible in post-freeze drone imagery. Recovery monitoring reveals species-specific resilience patterns.
Monitoring relevance
Allows quantification of freeze-induced canopy loss area and recovery timeline through sequential classification maps
🌀
Storm Surges & Hurricanes
Gulf coast tropical events introduce saltwater intrusion and mechanical damage to Roseau cane stands. Post-storm surveys document extent of physical disturbance and subsequent vegetation recovery dynamics.
Monitoring relevance
Before/after survey pairs isolate storm-driven habitat change from background seasonal variability
💧
Hydrological Fluctuations
Extreme water level variability — from record high levels in summer 2023 to complete site drying in fall seasons — profoundly affects both field survey access and vegetation condition across the study period.
Monitoring relevance
Multi-temporal comparison separates water-driven spectral change from true vegetation structural change
🌿
Gradual Environmental Change
Long-term subsidence, sediment dynamics, and salinity shifts in the lower MRD create cumulative stressors on Roseau cane habitat. Five-year monitoring captures trends invisible in single-season snapshots.
Monitoring relevance
Trend analysis across 5 years separates episodic disturbance from directional habitat change trajectory

Looking Ahead
The 5-Year Mid-Term Analysis

By the project's end, the complete time series will support a comprehensive habitat resilience assessment — linking drone-scale vegetation dynamics to field-observed disturbance events and environmental drivers.

Analysis 01
Temporal Change Detection
Area-based quantification of Roseau cane cover change across all five survey seasons. Isolating disturbance-driven loss from seasonal variability and long-term trends using multi-date classified map stack.
Analysis 02
Resilience Trajectory Mapping
Pixel-level tracking of class transitions (healthy Roseau cane → dead vegetation → recovery) to characterize resilience rates and identify areas of persistent die-back versus recovery success.
Analysis 03
Disturbance Attribution
Correlation of mapped habitat change with documented freeze events, storm occurrences, and hydrological records to attribute specific change episodes to environmental drivers.
Analysis 04
Management Recommendations
Synthesis of 5-year monitoring findings into spatially explicit conservation and restoration recommendations for Roseau cane habitat management in the lower MRD.
